The Rise of Hate Crimes in New York City

New York City, one of the most infamous places in the United States is known for its immense food, culture and diversity. The largest city in the U.S. does have one specific problem that has not been contained, especially recently: crime. The rise in hate crimes in New York City has substantially increased since pre-Covid up until now. Different minority groups are being targeted throughout all parts of New York City, with some counties being more dangerous than others. From 2019-2025, there were over 2000 felony hate crimes committed throughout all five of New York City’s counties. But which county is the most dangerous for minority groups?

Kings County, better known as Brooklyn, has had the most felony hate crimes recorded over the last six years with New York county not too far behind it. These two sectors of New York City are responsible for over 70% of hate crimes. Of these, Jews have been the primary target over the years. New York City is home to over 2 million jewish people, the most in any city in the U.S. and the 2nd most of any city in the world according to the Jewish Virtual Library.

Of the 2035 felony hate crimes committed across New York City since 2019, 1220 have been anti-semitic. Alexandra Lefkowitz, a NYC local, has lived through these rough times firsthand. “The rise in anti-semitism in New York City has been very upsetting, especially as a Jewish person. Attacks on synagogues, Jewish schools, and Jewish owned businesses make it hard to feel safe when celebrating the holidays with family and expressing our religion freely,” Lefkowitz said.

For the jewish local, a lot of these crimes have affected her on a personal level in her everyday life, from important days of celebration, to just an average day. These hate crimes have sparked different debates nationally and cause tensions in the city. One would think with such a high population of this group, that they would be a majority that would not undergo such hate. Despite nearly 60% of felony hate crimes in NYC being anti-semitic, there are also two other groups who have been targeted heavily: Male homosexuals and Asians.

Anti-male homosexual felonies in New York county are almost in the triple digits over the last 6 years. Of the 2035 felony hate crimes recorded since 2019, there have been nearly 200 accounts that have targeted this group, making it an unsafe environment for these individuals. New York County has been not only dangerous for male homosexuals and those of the jewish religion, but also Asians.

New York County was found responsible for exactly half of the anti-asian felony hate crimes committed and the majority of anti-male homosexual crimes. Although it seems as if New York County is the most dangerous by far, Kings County has nearly 100 more felony hate crimes on record over the past 6 years. The most populated city in the entire United States has been a victim of some of the highest rates of crimes, leaving several different groups negatively impacted.
