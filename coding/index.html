U.S. Troops in Egypt

1950 is when U.S. troops were first relovated to Egypt. It was not until the 1980s where the amount increased, and then again in the 1990s do to external affairs.

By Daniel Diez

In 1982, Operation Bright Star was founded. This operation, located relative to Alexandria, Egypt, was meant to enhance American ties in the Middle East. It sent American forces to Egypt to do training together. Many of the troops resided in different bases. Just a few years later in the early 90s, there would still be over 1,000 troops due to the Gulf War. In 1997, the large spike in troops is credited to Luxor Massacre. This explosion left 64 dead and 25 injured. It was the beginning of an internal violence between the government and its people. After that, the number of troops began to drop throughout the 2000s. Post 9/11, troops began to get relocated to different countries. Also with improvement of technology, there was not a necessity of so many troops to be in Egypt.
